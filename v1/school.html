I've been lucky to have some amazing mentors and teachers. This was because I was a part of a few really exceptional insitutions.

The Centre for Advanced Spatial Analysis

The Bartlett Faculty of the Built Environment: University College London: 2018-2019

CASA does a bunch of really cool stuff. I studied how modern quantitative methods from Data Science, Computer Science, Mathematics, Physics, and Geography can inform modern cities, their citizens, their planning, and their something else.

Macalester College

St Paul, MN: 2009-2013

I studied economics and chinese. It was really hard. I spent a lot of time with the rugby team, worked a few different student jobs and didn't get nearly enough sleep.

London School of Economics and Political Science

My junior year I spent at the LSE. I took the standard Macro-econ class and statistics for economics. The reason I was there though was to study The Economic History of China and The Philosophy of Economics I'd highly recommend both topics and can offer reading suggestions to anyone interested, though I think it would mean regaining access to an acient dropbox account that I used that year.

Albany High School

Despite its reputation AHS offered more AP classes than any other highschool in the Capital District and the opputunity to earn an international baccalaureate diploma, which I did as well as an advanced nys regents diploma. I was a competitive highschool rower and much less competitive high school swimmer. If you're a swimmer it might be informative to know that my preferred event was the 500.

St Pius X

I had some great teachers and friends at SPX. I learned the word "sovereignty" from Mr M and only was suspended once 😀.

Maria College Kindergarden

I saw the monarch butterflies start to emerge from their "chrysalises" in kindergarten. We had one of these parachute things and it was awesome.
